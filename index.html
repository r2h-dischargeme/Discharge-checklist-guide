Introduction
Your rehabilitation
journey starts here.

Your rehabilitation journey has been focused on helping you build confidence and skills for life at home. This guide is here to travel alongside you and your family as you prepare for this important transition.

Leaving rehabilitation is a significant milestone — one that can bring both excitement and uncertainty. This guide doesn't determine whether you're ready to go home; it's a supportive tool to help you reflect on different aspects of daily life and identify areas where you might need additional planning or support.

By working through this guide with your care team and loved ones, you'll have the opportunity to think through your readiness to:

  1. 01Look after your body and wellbeing
  2. 02Direct your own care with confidence
  3. 03Make informed choices about your health and lifestyle
  4. 04Continue your regular exercises and therapy plans
  5. 05Eat and drink well
  6. 06Engage in hobbies and activities that bring you joy
  7. 07Connect with family, friends, and your community
  8. 08Access transport and move safely in your community
  9. 09Get back to work or meaningful activities

This is your journey, and every person's path looks different. Take your time, be honest with yourself, and remember that your rehabilitation team is here to support you every step of the way.
